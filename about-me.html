Description

I am a junior at Bay Port High School with a passion for understanding how things operate and transforming ideas into something significant. A significant portion of my time is dedicated to creating projects and investigating new ideas that push my thinking. I have a keen interest in technology and problem-solving as they enable concepts to materialize in tangible, effective forms. Simultaneously, I feel that curiosity must be paired with humility and an openness to learning from others. Empathy and compassion are principles I strive to integrate into my interactions with others and how I tackle new situations. I appreciate settings that promote creativity, engaging dialogue, and ongoing development. Experiences both in school and beyond have influenced my perspective on challenges and opportunities. I aspire to continue learning and discovering ways to use what I create to make a meaningful and positive impact.

My 60 Seconds Assignment:

Instead of choosing to talk about myself for 60 seconds, I chose to ask the class some questions and have them guess aspects of my personality. At the end of each question, I revealed what the answer truly was and moved on to the next question. At the end of the presentation, I had each person in the class enter one word that described me after briefly learning about me, and all of the entries were displayed on the screen. In my presentation, I asked questions that focused on my personality and key traits rather than just hobbies and basic preferences. I included a variety of different topics that explored the bigger picture, including preferences related to collaboration and problem solving. This presentation not only engaged the audience in thinking critically about deeper personality traits I might or might not have, but also led them to draw conclusions based on the information they had just learned.
